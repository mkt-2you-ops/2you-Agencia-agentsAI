# 2YOU High-Output Agent Policy

## Purpose

This policy governs agents that can generate large amounts of output, publish autonomously, or expand channel complexity quickly.

Examples include:
- Growth Hacker
- Content Creator
- Social Media Strategist
- Carousel Growth Engine
- similar publishing, distribution, or automation-heavy agents

These agents are useful.
They are also dangerous when activated without strong upstream logic.

---

## Policy Statement

High-output agents may only be activated after the following are locked:
- objective
- offer
- ICP
- promise angle
- CTA
- KPI
- funnel role
- operating mode

If those are missing, route upward first.

---

## Mandatory Constraints

### 1. Role Constraint
Each high-output asset system must serve one primary role:
- awareness
- consideration
- objection handling
- conversion support
- retention
- authority support

### 2. Scope Constraint
Default to narrower channel and asset sets unless scale conditions are already proven.

### 3. Mode Constraint
- Caixa: direct response bias
- Validação: learning bias
- Escala: repeatability bias

### 4. Learning Constraint
Do not report vanity performance only.
Report business-relevant signal.

### 5. Escalation Constraint
High-output agents may recommend changes, but they may not unilaterally redefine:
- offer
- ICP
- business thesis
- scaling decision

Those changes go upward.

---

## Standard Activation Check

```markdown
# 2YOU High-Output Activation Check
- Objective locked: Yes / No
- Offer locked: Yes / No
- ICP locked: Yes / No
- CTA locked: Yes / No
- KPI locked: Yes / No
- Funnel role locked: Yes / No
- Mode chosen: Yes / No
- Autonomous publishing approved: Yes / No / Not applicable
```

If any critical field is missing, activation should fail.

---

## Why this exists

Because systems that can produce and distribute at scale often become extremely efficient at amplifying ambiguity.

That is impressive in a technical sense and disastrous in a business sense.
